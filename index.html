Inside The Exam · your bill

Before we touch the building, we take your bill apart. That's the exam.

Your bill isn't one number. It's energy, demand penalties, power-factor penalties, and quiet excess, and those dollars are not priced the same. The most punishing line is almost always your peak demand charge.1

So a smaller bill is never just "twenty percent off one system." Flatten the peak your building pulls and you move the most expensive dollars first, which is why the total can drop further than any single piece of equipment's percentage would suggest. We read every line, then file every deduction, credit, and rebate you qualify for.3, 4

Cash flow positive from day one on qualifying projects, structured right, and it only improves from there.1, 2

And if you ever find a better verified return than ours, we pay a finder's fee; the terms are in the disclosures below, in plain English.7

The waste doesn't just inflate the bill. It eats the equipment.

The same waste that shows up on your bill runs through the machines behind it. Three loads carry almost every building.

HVAC & RTU fleet

Compressors that never learned when to stop, working against coils that quietly fouled years ago. Running harder than the comfort requires ages an entire fleet years before its time.

$260K to $480K per property1

Motors & drives

Every fan, elevator, and pump rides on a motor, and every needless hard start wears it. On an elevator, the same quiet failure runs into six figures.

$2K to $20K per motor1

Pumps & circulation

Booster, chiller, and process pumps fail the same quiet way. A peak-season failure is the one that takes rooms, floors, or a production line down with it.

$8K to $25K per pump1

None of this appears in a standard energy audit. It survives for years, until someone runs The Exam on the building that's paying for it.
