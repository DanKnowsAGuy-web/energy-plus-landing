# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Health-serious adults who want precision, not one-size-fits-all protocols. Two overlapping
profiles: (1) people tired of conflicting dietary advice and generic supplement funnels,
struggling with fatigue, brain fog, or gut issues, who want clinical-grade data about their
own body they can act on; and (2) prospective or current GLP-1 users who want to do it
right, optimal dosage, timing, risk mitigation, and knowing when and how to cycle off,
guided by their actual biomarkers and genetics rather than guesswork. They arrive curious
but cautious, skeptical of hype and shady upsells, and often skew older with a real need
for readable, unhurried information. Context is evaluation: gauging legitimacy, cost, and
credibility before taking the first step. Job to be done: "show me this is real, precise,
and for me, then make enrolling feel safe and obvious."

## Product Purpose

A marketing / landing site for **Balance Protocol™ GPS**, a systems-biology program that
fuses functional lab biomarkers and clinically-relevant genetic SNP analysis (the
at-home **NutraCoreX™** lab array, 150+ biomarkers across 6 physiological categories) with
expert clinical interpretation. The combined offer presented here is one $998 journey:
the testing foundation AND the **GLP-1 optimization** it enables, dialing in dosage,
timing, cycling, and risk mitigation from each person's real biochemistry. Its job is to
convert visitors into enrollments. It exists because the metabolic-health and GLP-1
categories are crowded with five-gene "optimization" tests, fear-mongering, and supplement
upsells; a credible, premium presence that leads with data and zero pressure earns the
trust that converts. Success is qualified enrollments driven by an informed, low-risk
decision, not urgency.

## Brand Personality

Modern and premium. Confident, aspirational, design-forward wellness, with the
production value of a brand that takes itself seriously. Three words: assured,
refined, human. The voice is plainspoken and warm without being cute, authoritative
without being clinical. It should evoke calm confidence and a sense of "I'm in good
hands" rather than urgency or hype. Premium here means restraint and craft, not luxury
ornament.

## Anti-references

- **Generic telehealth (Hims / Ro look).** No muted-pastel, rounded-everything,
  interchangeable DTC-pharma sameness. Do not settle into the category-default look.
- **Crash-diet / weight-loss advertising.** No before/after photos, urgency banners,
  countdown pressure, miracle-cure language, or scammy-supplement energy.
- **Cold hospital / clinical.** Not sterile, intimidating, jargon-heavy, or
  insurance-portal ugly.
- **AI-generated SaaS template.** No cream/sand backgrounds, tiny uppercase tracked
  eyebrows above every section, identical icon-heading-text card grids, or gradient text.

## Design Principles

- **Earn trust before asking.** Credibility (clarity, evidence, real medical framing)
  precedes every conversion ask. The CTA wins because the page already convinced.
- **Calm over urgent.** Confidence converts better than pressure for this audience.
  No false scarcity; let the quality of the experience do the persuading.
- **Premium is restraint.** Distinction comes from craft, typography, space, and
  considered motion, not decoration. When in doubt, remove.
- **Readable as a feature.** Generous type, strong contrast, and unhurried pacing are
  brand assets for an older, evaluating audience, not accessibility afterthoughts.
- **Human, not category.** Speak like a trusted person, look like a brand with a point
  of view. Actively avoid every reflex of the GLP-1 / telehealth visual cliche.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Body text >=4.5:1 contrast (large text >=3:1), full keyboard
navigation, visible focus states, and a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for every
animation. Given the older / potentially lower-vision audience, bias toward generous
default type sizes and large tap targets even where AA does not strictly require it.
